## Summary of Key Findings

1. **Strongest parallel: EXO 21:24/LEV 24:20/DEU 19:21 <-> MAT 5:38.** Jesus directly quotes the lex talionis (civil penalty formula) and reinterprets it. This is the clearest evidence of NT engagement with OT civil law.

2. **DEU 4:13 <-> 2CO 3:3.** Paul's "tables of stone" reference confirms the Deuteronomy 4:13 distinction between the covenant (Ten Commandments on stone) and other legislation. The stone/Spirit contrast reinforces the moral law's enduring character.

3. **GEN 9:6 <-> 1CO 11:7/COL 1:15.** The "image of God" theological basis for civil law (Gen 9:6 is the pre-Mosaic blood-for-blood principle) finds NT echo in passages affirming the imago Dei.

4. **No direct NT citation of specific civil restitution formulas.** The theft laws (EXO 22:1-9), property laws, and servant laws do not appear to be directly quoted or cited in the NT.

5. **ROM 13:1-7 has no strong single OT parallel** -- it represents a general principle of divine sovereignty over civil authority rather than citing any specific OT civil law text.
