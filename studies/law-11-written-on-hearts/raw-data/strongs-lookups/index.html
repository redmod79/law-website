    Raw Strong's Lookups¶

    H8451 -- torah¶

    • 244 total occurrences, 47 unique translations
    • Lexicon: towrah (to-raw), feminine noun; from yarah; a precept or statute, especially the Decalogue or Pentateuch -- law.
    • Top translations: "the law" (60, 24.6%), "of the law" (28, 11.5%), "law" (24, 9.8%), "thy law" (20, 8.2%), "in the law" (19, 7.8%), "my law" (10, 4.1%)

    H3789 -- kathab¶

    • 248 total occurrences, 89 unique translations
    • Lexicon: kathab (kaw-thab), verb; a primitive root; to grave, by implication, to write.
    • Top translations: "written" (89, 35.9%), "wrote" (14, 5.6%), "write" (5, 2.0%)
    • "and I will write" = 1 occurrence: Exo 34:1

    H3820 -- leb¶

    • 649 total occurrences, 152 unique translations
    • Lexicon: leb (labe), masculine noun; the heart; used figuratively for feelings, will, intellect, centre of anything.
    • Top translations: "heart" (102, 15.7%), "my heart" (39, 6.0%), "his heart" (37, 5.7%)

    H3824 -- lebab¶

    • 267 total occurrences, 82 unique translations
    • Lexicon: lebab (lay-bawb), masculine noun; used also like the heart (as the most interior organ).
    • Top translations: "thine heart" (28, 10.5%), "heart" (24, 9.0%), "his heart" (22, 8.2%)

    H2706 -- choq¶

    • 134 total occurrences, 55 unique translations
    • Lexicon: choq (khoke), masculine noun; an enactment; hence, an appointment.
    • Top translations: "the statutes" (14, 10.4%), "thy statutes" (14, 10.4%), "statutes" (13, 9.7%), "my statutes" (4, 3.0%)

    H2708 -- chuqqah¶

    • 116 total occurrences, 40 unique translations
    • Lexicon: chuqqah (khook-kaw), feminine noun; feminine of choq, same meaning.
    • Top translations: "my statutes" (24, 20.7%), "a statute" (13, 11.2%), "in my statutes" (9, 7.8%)

    H4941 -- mishpat¶

    • 448 total occurrences, 133 unique translations
    • Lexicon: mishpat (mish-pawt), masculine noun; properly, a verdict; a sentence or formal decree; justice.
    • Top translations: "judgment" (69, 15.4%), "my judgments" (23, 5.1%), "judgments" (15, 3.3%)

    H1285 -- berith¶

    • 317 total occurrences, 45 unique translations
    • Lexicon: berith (ber-eeth), feminine noun; from barath (cutting); a compact (because made by passing between pieces of flesh).
    • Top translations: "of the covenant" (60, 18.9%), "my covenant" (45, 14.2%), "covenant" (40, 12.6%), "a covenant" (40, 12.6%)

    G3551 -- nomos¶

    • 169 total occurrences, 11 unique translations
    • Lexicon: nomos (nom'-os), masculine noun; from nemo (to parcel out).
    • Top translations: "law" (95, 56.2%), "the law" (49, 29.0%), "of the law" (14, 8.3%)

    G1345 -- dikaioma¶

    • 10 total occurrences, 6 unique translations
    • Lexicon: dikaioma (dik-ah'-yo-mah), neuter noun; an equitable deed; a statute or decision.
    • Translations: "ordinances" (3: Luk 1:6, Heb 9:1, Heb 9:10), "righteousness" (3: Rom 2:26, Rom 8:4, Rev 19:8), "judgment" (1: Rom 1:32), "justification" (1: Rom 5:16), "the righteousness" (1: Rom 5:18), "judgments" (1: Rev 15:4)

    G1378 -- dogma¶

    • 5 total occurrences, 4 unique translations
    • Lexicon: dogma (dog'-mah), neuter noun; from dokeo; a law (civil, ceremonial or ecclesiastical).
    • Translations: "decrees" (2: Act 16:4, Act 17:7), "a decree" (1: Luk 2:1), "ordinances" (1: Eph 2:15), "of ordinances" (1: Col 2:14)
    • NEVER used for the Decalogue. Only 2 of 5 uses are religious (Eph 2:15, Col 2:14); the rest are civil.

    G2673 -- katargeo¶

    • 32 total occurrences, 30 unique translations
    • Lexicon: katargeo (kat-arg-eh'-o), verb; from kata + argeo; to be (render) entirely idle (useless).
    • Key verses: Rom 3:31 ("make void"), 2 Cor 3:7,11,13 ("done away"/"abolished"), Eph 2:15 ("abolished"), Gal 3:17 ("of none effect")

    H3772 -- karath¶

    • 358 total occurrences, 156 unique translations
    • Lexicon: karath (kaw-rath), verb; a primitive root; to cut (off, down or asunder); by implication, to destroy or consume; specifically, to covenant (i.e. make an alliance or bargain, originally by cutting flesh and passing between the pieces).
    • Top translations: "shall be cut off" (30, 8.4%), "made" (18, 5.0%), "and cut off" (12, 3.4%), "he made" (8, 2.2%), "cut off" (8, 2.2%)
    • Key covenant uses: Jer 31:31 ("I will make a new covenant"), Deu 5:2-3 ("The LORD our God made a covenant"), Exo 34:27 ("I have made a covenant")

    H2710 -- chaqaq¶

    • 19 total occurrences, 18 unique translations
    • Lexicon: chaqaq (khaw-kak), verb; a primitive root; properly, to hack, i.e. engrave; by implication, to enact (laws being cut in stone or metal tablets in primitive times) or generally prescribe.
    • Top translations: "my lawgiver" (2), "decree" (1), "the law" (1), "they were printed" (1), "I have graven thee" (1)
    • Root behind choq (H2706) and chuqqah (H2708). Etymological meaning connects engraving/inscribing to law-giving.

    G1343 -- dikaiosyne¶

    • 80 total occurrences, 12 unique translations
    • Lexicon: dikaiosyne (dik-ah-yos-oo-nay), feminine noun; from dikaios; equity (of character or act); specially (Christian) justification.
    • Top translations: "righteousness" (42, 52.5%), "of righteousness" (18, 22.5%), "the righteousness" (9, 11.2%)
    • Distinct from dikaioma (G1345): dikaiosyne = abstract quality/state; dikaioma = concrete requirement/deed.

    G3548 -- nomothesia¶

    • 1 total occurrence, 1 unique translation
    • Lexicon: nomothesia (nom-oth-es-ee-ah), feminine noun; from nomotheteo; legislation (specially, the institution of the Mosaic code).
    • Single occurrence: Rom 9:4 ("giving of the law")

    H6662 -- tsaddiyq¶

    • 210 total occurrences, 33 unique translations
    • Lexicon: tsaddiyq (tsad-deek), adjective; from tsadaq; just, lawful, righteous (man).
    • Top translations: "the righteous" (49, 23.3%), "of the righteous" (38, 18.1%), "righteous" (27, 12.9%), "just" (12, 5.7%)

    H6666 -- tsedaqah¶

    • 165 total occurrences, 57 unique translations
    • Lexicon: tsedaqah (tsed-aw-kaw), feminine noun; from tsadaq; rightness (abstractly), subjectively (rectitude), objectively (justice), morally (virtue) or figuratively (prosperity).
    • Top translations: "righteousness" (19, 11.5%), "and righteousness" (10, 6.1%), "of righteousness" (9, 5.5%), "his righteousness" (8, 4.8%)
    • Key connection: Isa 51:6-7 links "my righteousness shall not be abolished" with "the people in whose heart is my law"
