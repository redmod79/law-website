    Question¶

    What do entole, nomos, dogma, cheirographon, and dikaioma reveal about law categories in the NT?

    A. Entole (G1785) Passages -- All NT Occurrences¶

    A1. Entole for Decalogue / Moral Commands¶

    Matthew 5:19 -- Jesus on commandments

    Whosoever therefore shall break one of these least commandments [G1785], and shall teach men so, he shall be called the least in the kingdom of heaven: but whosoever shall do and teach [them], the same shall be called great in the kingdom of heaven.

    Matthew 15:3 -- God's commandment vs. tradition

    But he answered and said unto them, Why do ye also transgress the commandment [G1785] of God by your tradition?

    Matthew 15:6 -- Commandment made void

    And honour not his father or his mother, [he shall be free]. Thus have ye made the commandment [G1785] of God of none effect by your tradition.

    Matthew 19:17-19 -- Keep the commandments = Decalogue

    And he said unto him, Why callest thou me good? [there is] none good but one, [that is], God: but if thou wilt enter into life, keep the commandments [G1785]. He saith unto him, Which? Jesus said, Thou shalt do no murder, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Honour thy father and [thy] mother: and, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.

    Matthew 22:36-40 -- Greatest commandment

    Master, which [is] the great commandment [G1785] in the law? Jesus said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind. This is the first and great commandment [G1785]. And the second [is] like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. On these two commandments [G1785] hang all the law and the prophets.

    Mark 7:8-9 -- Commandment of God vs. tradition of men

    For laying aside the commandment [G1785] of God, ye hold the tradition of men, [as] the washing of pots and cups: and many other such like things ye do. And he said unto them, Full well ye reject the commandment [G1785] of God, that ye may keep your own tradition.

    Mark 10:19 -- Commandments = Decalogue listed

    Thou knowest the commandments [G1785], Do not commit adultery, Do not kill, Do not steal, Do not bear false witness, Defraud not, Honour thy father and mother.

    Mark 12:28-31 -- First commandment

    And one of the scribes came, and having heard them reasoning together, and perceiving that he had answered them well, asked him, Which is the first commandment [G1785] of all? And Jesus answered him, The first of all the commandments [G1785] [is], Hear, O Israel; The Lord our God is one Lord: And thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind, and with all thy strength: this [is] the first commandment [G1785]. And the second [is] like, [namely] this, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. There is none other commandment [G1785] greater than these.

    Luke 18:20 -- Commandments = Decalogue

    Thou knowest the commandments [G1785], Do not commit adultery, Do not kill, Do not steal, Do not bear false witness, Honour thy father and thy mother.

    Luke 23:56 -- Resting according to the commandment

    And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments; and rested the sabbath day according to the commandment [G1785].

    Romans 7:7-13 -- The commandment identified as the 10th commandment

    What shall we say then? [Is] the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet. But sin, taking occasion by the commandment [G1785], wrought in me all manner of concupiscence. For without the law sin [was] dead. For I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment [G1785] came, sin revived, and I died. And the commandment [G1785], which [was ordained] to life, I found [to be] unto death. For sin, taking occasion by the commandment [G1785], deceived me, and by it slew [me]. Wherefore the law [is] holy, and the commandment [G1785] holy, and just, and good. Was then that which is good made death unto me? God forbid. But sin, that it might appear sin, working death in me by that which is good; that sin by the commandment [G1785] might become exceeding sinful.

    Romans 13:9 -- Decalogue commands called entole

    For this, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet; and if [there be] any other commandment [G1785], it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.

    Ephesians 6:2-3 -- Fifth commandment cited as binding

    Honour thy father and mother; (which is the first commandment [G1785] with promise;) That it may be well with thee, and thou mayest live long on the earth.

    1 Corinthians 7:19 -- Entole distinguished from circumcision

    Circumcision is nothing, and uncircumcision is nothing, but the keeping of the commandments [G1785] of God.

    A2. Entole in Johannine Literature¶

    John 14:15 -- Keep my commandments

    If ye love me, keep my commandments [G1785].

    John 14:21 -- Having and keeping commandments

    He that hath my commandments [G1785], and keepeth them, he it is that loveth me: and he that loveth me shall be loved of my Father, and I will love him, and will manifest myself to him.

    John 15:10 -- Father's commandments and Jesus's commandments

    If ye keep my commandments [G1785], ye shall abide in my love; even as I have kept my Father's commandments [G1785], and abide in his love.

    John 15:12 -- This is my commandment

    This is my commandment [G1785], That ye love one another, as I have loved you.

    1 John 2:3-4 -- Knowing God = keeping commandments

    And hereby we do know that we know him, if we keep his commandments [G1785]. He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his commandments [G1785], is a liar, and the truth is not in him.

    1 John 2:7-8 -- Old and new commandment

    Brethren, I write no new commandment [G1785] unto you, but an old commandment [G1785] which ye had from the beginning. The old commandment [G1785] is the word which ye have heard from the beginning. Again, a new commandment [G1785] I write unto you, which thing is true in him and in you: because the darkness is past, and the true light now shineth.

    1 John 3:22-24 -- Keep his commandments

    And whatsoever we ask, we receive of him, because we keep his commandments [G1785], and do those things that are pleasing in his sight. And this is his commandment [G1785], That we should believe on the name of his Son Jesus Christ, and love one another, as he gave us commandment [G1785]. And he that keepeth his commandments [G1785] dwelleth in him, and he in him.

    1 John 4:21 -- Commandment to love brother

    And this commandment [G1785] have we from him, That he who loveth God love his brother also.

    1 John 5:2-3 -- Commandments not grievous

    By this we know that we love the children of God, when we love God, and keep his commandments [G1785]. For this is the love of God, that we keep his commandments [G1785]: and his commandments [G1785] are not grievous.

    2 John 1:4-6 -- Walking in commandments

    I rejoiced greatly that I found of thy children walking in truth, as we have received a commandment [G1785] from the Father. And now I beseech thee, lady, not as though I wrote a new commandment [G1785] unto thee, but that which we had from the beginning, that we love one another. And this is love, that we walk after his commandments [G1785].

    A3. Entole in Revelation (End-time Saints)¶

    Revelation 12:17 -- Commandments of God

    And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments [G1785] of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.

    Revelation 14:12 -- Patience of the saints

    Here is the patience of the saints: here [are] they that keep the commandments [G1785] of God, and the faith of Jesus.

    Revelation 22:14 -- Blessed are they that do his commandments

    Blessed [are] they that do his commandments [G1785], that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city.

    A4. Entole for Ceremonial/Levitical (with qualifiers)¶

    Hebrews 7:5 -- Commandment to take tithes

    And verily they that are of the sons of Levi, who receive the office of the priesthood, have a commandment [G1785] to take tithes of the people according to the law, that is, of their brethren, though they come out of the loins of Abraham.

    Hebrews 7:16 -- Carnal commandment

    Who is made, not after the law of a carnal commandment [G1785], but after the power of an endless life.

    Hebrews 7:18 -- Disannulling of the commandment

    For there is verily a disannulling of the commandment [G1785] going before for the weakness and unprofitableness thereof.

    Hebrews 9:19 -- Every precept spoken by Moses

    For when Moses had spoken every precept [G1785] to all the people according to the law, he took the blood of calves and of goats, with water, and scarlet wool, and hyssop, and sprinkled both the book, and all the people.

    A5. Entole -- Other Uses¶

    Luke 1:6 -- Entole and dikaioma side by side

    And they were both righteous before God, walking in all the commandments [G1785] and ordinances [G1345] of the Lord blameless.

    Luke 15:29 -- Son's complaint

    And he answering said to [his] father, Lo, these many years do I serve thee, neither transgressed I at any time thy commandment [G1785].

    Colossians 4:10 -- Apostolic commandments

    Aristarchus my fellowprisoner saluteth you, and Marcus, sister's son to Barnabas, (touching whom ye received commandments [G1785]: if he come unto you, receive him;)

    Titus 1:14 -- Commandments of men

    Not giving heed to Jewish fables, and commandments [G1785] of men, that turn from the truth.

    1 Timothy 6:14 -- Keep this commandment

    That thou keep [this] commandment [G1785] without spot, unrebukeable, until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ.

    Ephesians 2:15 -- CRITICAL: Entole qualified by en dogmasin

    Having abolished in his flesh the enmity, [even] the law of commandments [G1785] [contained] in ordinances [G1378]; for to make in himself of twain one new man, [so] making peace.

    B. All Dogma (G1378) Passages -- Complete Set (5 occurrences)¶

    Luke 2:1 -- Caesar's decree (civil/imperial)

    And it came to pass in those days, that there went out a decree [G1378] from Caesar Augustus, that all the world should be taxed.

    Acts 16:4 -- Jerusalem Council decrees (ecclesiastical)

    And as they went through the cities, they delivered them the decrees [G1378] for to keep, that were ordained of the apostles and elders which were at Jerusalem.

    Acts 17:7 -- Caesar's decrees (civil/imperial)

    Whom Jason hath received: and these all do contrary to the decrees [G1378] of Caesar, saying that there is another king, [one] Jesus.

    Ephesians 2:15 -- Law of commandments in ordinances (ABOLISHED)

    Having abolished in his flesh the enmity, [even] the law of commandments [G1785] [contained] in ordinances [G1378]; for to make in himself of twain one new man, [so] making peace.

    Colossians 2:14 -- Handwriting of ordinances (NAILED TO CROSS)

    Blotting out the handwriting [G5498] of ordinances [G1378] that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross.

    Dogmatizo (G1379) -- Verbal form (1 occurrence)¶

    Colossians 2:20 -- Subject to ordinances

    Wherefore if ye be dead with Christ from the rudiments of the world, why, as though living in the world, are ye subject to ordinances [G1379],

    Context (Col 2:21-23):

    (Touch not; taste not; handle not; Which all are to perish with the using;) after the commandments and doctrines of men? Which things have indeed a shew of wisdom in will worship, and humility, and neglecting of the body; not in any honour to the satisfying of the flesh.

    C. Cheirographon (G5498) -- Col 2:14 (Hapax Legomenon)¶

    Colossians 2:13-17 -- Full context

    And you, being dead in your sins and the uncircumcision of your flesh, hath he quickened together with him, having forgiven you all trespasses; Blotting out the handwriting [G5498] of ordinances [G1378] that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross; [And] having spoiled principalities and powers, he made a shew of them openly, triumphing over them in it. Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath [days]: Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body [is] of Christ.

    Colossians 2:20-22 -- Continuation

    Wherefore if ye be dead with Christ from the rudiments of the world, why, as though living in the world, are ye subject to ordinances [G1379], (Touch not; taste not; handle not; Which all are to perish with the using;) after the commandments and doctrines of men?

    D. All Dikaioma (G1345) Passages -- Complete Set (10 occurrences)¶

    Luke 1:6 -- Entole and dikaioma as two categories

    And they were both righteous before God, walking in all the commandments [G1785] and ordinances [G1345] of the Lord blameless.

    Romans 1:32 -- Judgment/righteous decree of God

    Who knowing the judgment [G1345] of God, that they which commit such things are worthy of death, not only do the same, but have pleasure in them that do them.

    Romans 2:26 -- Righteousness of the law

    Therefore if the uncircumcision keep the righteousness [G1345] of the law, shall not his uncircumcision be counted for circumcision?

    Romans 5:16 -- Justification (forensic)

    And not as [it was] by one that sinned, [so is] the gift: for the judgment [was] by one to condemnation, but the free gift [is] of many offences unto justification [G1345].

    Romans 5:18 -- Righteousness of one
